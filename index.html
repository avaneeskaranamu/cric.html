About cricket

Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of eleven players each on a field at the centre of which is a 22-yard pitch with a wicket at each end, each comprising two bails balanced on three stumps.



International governing body of cricket!

The International Cricket Council is the world governing body of cricket. It was founded as the Imperial Cricket Conference in 1909 by representatives from Australia, England and South Africa. It was renamed as the International Cricket Conference in 1965, and took up its current name in 1987. Chairman: Greg Barclay

CEO: Geoff Allardice

Headquarters: Dubai, United Arab Emirates

Founded: June 15, 1909
Abbreviation: ICC Membership: 106* members
